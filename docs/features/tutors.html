Meet the Tutors

Each subject has its own AI personality. Not generic assistants — real characters that kids enjoy talking to.

🧮

Matteo

Mathematics

Patient, encouraging, never frustrated. Uses everyday examples to make abstract concepts click.

  • Explains step-by-step
  • "Stell dir vor, du kaufst 3 Pokémon-Packs..."
  • Celebrates small wins
  • Never gives answers directly
🇬🇧

Nelly

English

Cool and casual. Naturally mixes German and English. Makes vocabulary feel like unlocking a secret code.

  • Code-switches naturally
  • "Das word 'awesome' is basically..."
  • Pop culture references
  • Builds confidence gradually
🎹

Melody

Music Theory

Enthusiastic about music. Teaches harmony through a Korg Monologue synthesizer with MIDI feedback.

  • Chords as arpeggios (mono synth!)
  • Bach as teaching material
  • Real-time practice feedback
  • Ear training exercises

/mathe
Hey! Matteo hier 🧮 Was rechnen wir?
Nelly
Hey! Nelly here 🇬🇧 What's up?

Why Personalities Matter

Generic AI assistants feel... generic. Kids don't engage with them long-term.

But characters work differently:

  • Consistency — Same voice every time builds trust
  • Relatability — Age-appropriate language and references
  • Engagement — Kids actually enjoy the interaction
  • Boundaries — Character constraints prevent abuse

Create Your Own

Each tutor is defined by a simple markdown file (MATTEO.md, NELLY.md, etc.). You can:

  • Adjust personality and teaching style
  • Add new subjects (Geography, Science, ...)
  • Customize language and tone for your kids
  • Add subject-specific example dialogues
