Adaptive Learning Types

Every child learns differently. ClawTutor detects their style and adapts — automatically.

The VARK Model

Four learning styles, four different ways to explain the same concept.

🖼️

Visual

Learns through images, diagrams, and spatial understanding.

  • ASCII diagrams and number lines
  • "Picture this..." explanations
  • Structured layouts with clear sections
  • Mind maps and flowcharts
💬

Auditory

Learns through listening, discussing, and explaining out loud.

  • "Explain it back to me" prompts
  • Concepts rephrased multiple ways
  • Encourages talking through problems
  • More questions, fewer statements
📝

Read/Write

Learns through reading text, lists, and written instructions.

  • Clear definitions first
  • Bullet-point summaries
  • Step-by-step written guides
  • Encourages note-taking
🛠️

Kinesthetic

Learns through doing, examples, and real-world application.

  • Starts with real scenarios
  • "Imagine you're at the store..."
  • Trial and error encouraged
  • Lots of practice problems

How It Works

1

Quick Onboarding

On first contact, the tutor asks 4 simple, playful questions. No formal test — just conversation.

2

Profile Created

The answers determine primary and secondary learning types. Stored in the child's workspace.

3

Adapted Teaching

Every explanation is tailored. Same concept, different delivery — matched to how they learn best.

4

Continuous Learning

The tutor observes what works. Notes are added to the profile over time for even better personalization.

Same Concept, Different Approach

Here's how the tutor explains "dividing 12 by 3" to different learners:

🛠️ Kinesthetic

"Imagine you have 12 stickers and want to share them equally with 2 friends. How many does each person get?"

📝 Read/Write

"Division rule: dividend ÷ divisor = quotient. Here: 12 ÷ 3 = 4. The dividend is what you're splitting up."

🖼️ Visual

"Picture 12 dots: ●●●● | ●●●● | ●●●● — see the three groups? Each group has 4."

💬 Auditory

"If I asked you to split 12 things three ways, how would you figure it out? Walk me through your thinking."
